Almost every Soraka player I see takes heal every single game without even thinking twice about it. And honestly, I get it. It feels safe, it feels reliable, and it works in most games, especially on a healer like Soraka. But there is one summoner spell that most Soraka players never seriously consider. And in many situations, it is just simply way, way better. Today, I'm going to break down exactly when and why you should take exhaust and when to take heal. By the end of this video, you will never have to guess again. Before I tell you when to take exhaust, I want to be clear. Heal is great on Soraka. It's not a bad choice. There are multiple reasons why it works so well on her. First, heal gives both you and your ADC a good burst of health and movement speed. That is great for aggressive playstyle winning trades, as well as running away from the enemy. Second, heal also heals Soraka for a good amount health. That extra health means more HP to spend on your W. More W casts means more healing for your whole team. Lastly, because Soraka stacks heal percentage items, heal scales better on her than most other supports. The more heal percent you build, the stronger your heal summoner gets. So yes, heal is good. It makes sense why people take it. That's not the issue. The issue is that most Soraka players take heal automatically without ever asking themselves if there is a better option. They open champ select, choose heal out of habit, and never consider taking exhaust instead. There are specific situations, specific champions, specific compositions where exhaust is just flat-out better. If you never think about it, you go into those games already at a huge disadvantage before the game has even started. So, when specifically should you take exhaust? Here are a few good examples. First, versus all-in ADCs that can easily run you down both in lane and outside of lane phase. Champions like Draven, Samira, Vayne, Kaisa, who can chase you across the map and burst you before you can escape. Exhaust massively slows their chase and softens the burst. It is especially a must-have versus strong one-shot combos like, for example, Lucian Nami or Leona Draven. It buys you way more time to cast more Ws on your ADC, as well as higher chances to land your Q. Just remember, this is very important. You can't be the one who gets engaged on. Your ADC gets engaged on, you put exhaust on the enemy ADC, and heal your own ADC as much as you can. Also, take note that whenever enemy picks engage support, they usually take ignite. It makes picking up heal even worse, unless you can use your heal reliably before enemy ignites your ADC. Second, versus jungle assassins like Nocturne, Rengar, Zed, or Talon jungle. They will appear from nowhere specifically to one-shot you. Exhaust is your only real answer to situations like this. Illaoi sticks also falls into this category. Third, versus mid lane assassins like Zed, Fizz, Qiyana, Talon, Katarina, Naafiri. They always find you in team fights. Same logic, they come for you, exhaust keeps you alive long enough to react or do something. One thing to note, versus Zed, Katarina, or anything combo reliant that can jump on you, you can always hold your E silence as they jump on you with R instead of taking exhaust. So, use your judgment in these [music] cases. Next one, versus top lane bruisers that can easily run at you, like Garen, Riven, Darius. If they reach you in a team fight, they chunk or kill you fast. Exhaust softens the hit and slows them down. Some bruisers are easier to kite with your Q or E, so it is always your call whether to take it. Lastly, versus berserkers like Master Yi or Olaf. They love flanking your team most of the times, and exhaust nicely counters both of them. It cuts their damage output massively during their ultimates, and it is one of the few reliable answers to them diving directly onto you. When not to pick exhaust, you may ask. Exhaust has one huge weakness, range. You need to be close to use it. And if the enemy team is mostly long-ranged or poke heavy, you will rarely be in a position to cast it effectively. Think of a composition with a lot of long-range champions, for example, Caitlyn ADC, Nami support, Xerath mid lane. In such scenarios, heal is a better call because the extra healing across a long poke heavy game will be more useful than a summoner spell that you can almost never use. Here is the thing that most people get wrong about exhaust on Soraka. You are not taking it for your ADC, not for any of your teammates. You are taking it for yourself only. Soraka is one of the most targeted champions in the game. And if you die, your team loses most of their healing. Good players know this, and they will send someone specifically to delete you in every single team fight. An assassin jumping on you, a bruiser running you down, an engage support locking you up. These players are not going for your ADC. They are coming for you. Exhaust is your answer to that. It is a self-preservation tool, not a peel tool for your carry. Once you understand that, everything about when to take it becomes obvious. Let's go through few lobby examples on when you should take exhaust and when you should take heal instead. In this one, enemy Talon and Diana can easily jump at you, and you do not have much counterplay. That's why we are taking exhaust. They are both melee champions, so using exhaust on them won't be a problem. The other high burst champion is Gangplank, but most of the times we will not be in range to use exhaust on him. In another lobby, champions that can quite easily jump at us are Zac and Poppy. Both of these champions can easily trigger Yasuo ultimate as well, which creates another huge threat. We should definitely take exhaust in this one. In the next one, we've got two bruisers that can easily one-shot us, Riven and Jax, on jungle an AP assassin and Draven on ADC. For you, it is to decide if you can space and kite properly champions like Riven and Jax, but you still should definitely take it against Evelynn and Draven. Holding your silence against these four champions will also help. In the last one, we've got only one champion that we can reliably exhaust. For me, Master Yi is always a huge threat, and I always take it whenever I face him on enemy team. You do have to be aware, though, that in lane versus Rakan and Xayah, your exhaust will not be as useful as heal would be. It is for you to decide if heal here is better, as you are taking exhaust only for Master Yi. So, when do you take heal? Here is the simple version. Default to heal when there is no clear exhaust target in the enemy composition. If you look at their team and nobody is going to specifically run you down or dive you, heal is fine. Versus ranged or poke heavy compositions where you cannot safely get close enough to use exhaust. And remember, heal is still excellent on Soraka specifically because of heal percentage scaling. In the right games, it is genuinely the stronger choice. The truth is that after more games, you will start reading the enemy composition in champ select and just know which summoner spell to take. But until then, ask yourself these two questions in every lobby. First, is there someone on their team that is going to come for me specifically? And will I have any trouble kiting and spacing them with my Q and E? If yes, take exhaust. If no, heal is fine. That one spell change will save you way more games than any rune page or item change.
